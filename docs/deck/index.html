01 · Why now

In a disaster, the network is the first casualty — and every rescue app assumes it's still there.

Cell towers fail within hours — usually from power, not damage. Exactly when people need to call for help, they can't.

Act III · the responder

One tap — like a ride request. Accept.

The nearest responder gets the top victim. Accept, and the loop closes: the operator sees en route, other teams see the sector is taken, and the victim hears — in her language — that help is coming. No double-searched buildings, no radio chatter.

02 · It already worked — and it keeps happening
Kerala floods · 2018
15,000

people located — and 1,800+ rescued — by ~300 volunteers with amateur radios, from one room, when every network was down. Improvised, no AI. It still saved lives. We made it a system.

The honest limit

In India a satellite messenger is illegal — travellers have been arrested carrying one. There's no legal, affordable off-grid alternative. We don't replace towers or fix governance; we cover the hours before help arrives.
