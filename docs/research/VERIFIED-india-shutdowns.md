## Claim 2 — Kashmir (J&K) 2019 internet shutdown was the longest ever in a democracy (~18 months / ~550 days)

### Verdict: CONFIRMED

**Exact dates:**
- **Start:** August 5, 2019 (revocation of Article 370)
- **4G/broadband restoration:** February 5, 2021

**Duration:** August 5, 2019 to February 5, 2021 = **1 year, 6 months, 1 day (approximately 549–552 days)**

**Figures in circulation:**
- "552 consecutive days" — cited by Access Now and Rest of World (https://restofworld.org/2024/india-internet-shutdown-record/)
- "550 days" — cited by HRW (2023 report)
- Wikipedia records the shutdown as lasting from "5 August 2019 – 5 February 2021" = ~549 days

The slight variation (549–552) reflects different calculation methods or precise restoration endpoints. **The "~550 days" figure is defensible and well-sourced; the "~18 months" framing is also accurate.**

**Longest in a democracy claim:**
- Access Now (December 2019): "As of December 16, J&K's ongoing internet shutdown has become the longest-ever internet shutdown carried out in a democratic state." (Cited via The Swaddle: https://www.theswaddle.com/indias-internet-shutdown-in-jk-is-now-the-longest-imposed-in-any-democracy)
- IFJ documented milestone at 365 days: https://www.ifj.org/media-centre/news/detail/article/india-a-grim-milestone-365-days-of-internet-shutdown-in-kashmir
- ORF (Observer Research Foundation): https://www.orfonline.org/expert-speak/how-the-worlds-longest-internet-shutdown-has-failed-to-counter-extremism-in-kashmir

**Direct quotes:**
- Access Now: "the longest-ever internet shutdown carried out in a democratic state"
- Rest of World: "Jammu and Kashmir experienced 552 consecutive days of internet blackout in August 2019, documented as the world's longest shutdown on record."

**Best source URLs:**
- Wikipedia (dates): https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019%E2%80%932021_Jammu_and_Kashmir_lockdown
- Access Now (democracy claim): https://www.accessnow.org/after-a-partial-restoration-of-internet-access-in-jammu-kashmir-access-now-urges-full-access/

**Confidence: HIGH**

## Claim 3 — Manipur 2023 ethnic violence internet shutdown lasted months (~200+ days)

### Verdict: CONFIRMED

**Timeline:**
- Start: May 3, 2023 (coinciding with Kuki-Meitei ethnic clashes)
- Duration of longest/first shutdown: **over 210 days** (through ~December 2023)
- Total shutdown orders in 2023: **18 orders** (The News Minute)
- Total blockade hours in 2023: **4,374 hours** (from search data)

**Scope:** Approximately 3.2 million people in Manipur endured a statewide shutdown lasting 212 days (Access Now 2023 KIO report).

**Scale in context:** This is "the longest such shutdown in the state's history and one of the longest in democratic India after Jammu & Kashmir." (Imphal Times editorial)

**Exact supportable wording:**
"In May 2023, amid ethnic violence between Meitei and Kuki-Zomi communities, Manipur's government imposed an internet shutdown that lasted over 210 days — affecting approximately 3.2 million people and running from May through late 2023."

**Best source URLs:**
- The News Minute (18 orders): https://www.thenewsminute.com/news/manipur-witnessed-18-internet-shutdown-orders-2023-179990
- Access Now 2023 KIO report (3.2M people, 212 days): https://www.accessnow.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/2023-KIO-Report.pdf

**Confidence: HIGH**

## Claim 4 — Documented case of shutdown impeding disaster/emergency response within India

### Verdict: CONFIRMED (strongest case: Kashmir 2019, with peer-reviewed medical corroboration; secondary: Manipur 2023)

### PRIMARY CASE: Kashmir 2019 — HRW-documented healthcare emergencies

**Source:** Human Rights Watch, "Kashmir Shutdown Raises Healthcare Concerns," August 30, 2019  
**URL:** https://www.hrw.org/news/2019/08/30/kashmir-shutdown-raises-healthcare-concerns

**Documented specific incidents:**

1. **Miscarriage / stillbirth:** A Kashmiri journalist reported his sister suffered a miscarriage because the communications ban "prevented real time communication to the senior gynecologist that could have saved the baby" (HRW, August 30, 2019). A separate case on August 9 recorded a stillborn birth after parents had to walk to a district hospital due to transport suspension.

2. **Ambulance inaccessible:** One mother reported she could not summon an ambulance when her daughter developed appendix pain in Pampore, 10 miles from Srinagar. They traveled in a load carrier and were stopped at more than seven barricades (HRW).

3. **Cancer patient:** A 63-year-old cancer patient had "no means to call an ambulance" during the shutdown (HRW / multiple reports).

4. **Emergency coordination breakdown:** A government doctor stated: "You could not reach the fire station, ambulance, or police. There was complete chaos." (HRW 2023 comprehensive report: https://www.hrw.org/report/2023/06/14/no-internet-means-no-work-no-pay-no-food/internet-shutdowns-deny-access-basic)

5. **COVID-19 public health impeded:** During the pandemic, the ongoing internet restrictions (only 2G restored in January 2020, 4G not until February 2021) made it "impossible to upload videos, upload information on COVID-19" for remote health outreach (HRW 2023 report).

### SECONDARY CASE: Manipur 2023 — peer-reviewed medical literature

**Source:** Jyothi S, Dhali S, Akoijam BS, Devi PA. "Effect of internet shutdown on postgraduate trainees in Manipur: A qualitative study." *Indian Journal of Medical Research*, December 2024; 160(6):630–636. DOI: 10.25259/IJMR_206_2024  
**URL:** https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39913521/

**Findings:** The shutdown "affected patient care such as seeking timely opinions from consultants, delays in sharing patient reports and inconveniences in the hand-off process" at tertiary care hospitals. (21 in-depth interviews with postgraduate trainees at two hospitals.)

**Caution:** This is a qualitative study of postgraduate trainees, not a documented death toll or formal emergency incident report. It confirms system-level harm to hospital care but is not a documented specific emergency fatality in Manipur.

### ADDITIONAL: Manipur flood/disaster overlap

The Imphal Times editorial states the shutdown "hampered disaster response during the recent floods, as information sharing was delayed and relief coordination became chaotic." This is asserted in commentary but not backed by a primary incident report — **treat as corroborating context, not a standalone citable incident.**

**Most defensible pitch framing:**
"During Kashmir's 2019 shutdown, Human Rights Watch documented that residents could not call ambulances, a pregnant woman's miscarriage was worsened because doctors couldn't reach a senior gynecologist, and a government doctor described 'complete chaos' in emergency coordination. A peer-reviewed 2024 study in the Indian Journal of Medical Research found the 2023 Manipur shutdown delayed patient care at tertiary hospitals — with trainees reporting delays in sharing reports and consulting specialists."

**Confidence: HIGH for Kashmir; MEDIUM-HIGH for Manipur**

## Claim 5 — Overlap between India's shutdown-prone and disaster-prone regions

### Verdict: CONFIRMED

**Shutdown-prone states** (top states per Access Now and SFLC.in data):
- Jammu and Kashmir (historically #1 by far — 19 shutdowns by 2016 alone)
- Manipur (36 shutdowns in 2023 alone; top state 2023–2024)
- Haryana, Rajasthan (exam/protest-linked)

**Disaster exposure of these same states:**

**Jammu and Kashmir:**
- Flood/landslide: The 2014 J&K floods were the worst in 60 years. J&K lies in the Himalayas — flash floods, glacial lake outburst floods (GLOFs), and landslides are recurrent.
- Seismic: J&K falls in Seismic Zone IV–V (high to very high risk).

**Manipur:**
- Flood/landslide: Confirmed flood damage in 2023 during the same period as the shutdown (900+ houses damaged in Manipur/Mizoram per search results).
- Seismic: Manipur is in Seismic Zone V (highest risk) — confirmed by NITI Aayog Natural Disaster Vulnerability mapping and WION seismic zone report.

**Best source URLs:**
- NITI Aayog disaster vulnerability: https://iced.niti.gov.in/climate-and-environment/environment/natural-disaster/
- Seismic zones (BMTPC map): https://www.bmtpc.org/datafiles/cms/file/map%20of%20india/eq-india.pdf
- Northeast flood/landslide: https://iasscore.in/current-affairs/floods-and-landslides-in-northeast-india

**Confidence: HIGH (seismic zone overlap is official government data; flood overlap is well-documented)**

## Key caveats for the pitch

1. **2024 ranking:** India dropped to #2 globally (84 vs Myanmar's 85) for the first time in six years. The safer claim for 2024 is "most shutdowns among democracies" — India remained #1 among democratic nations in 2024.

2. **"552 days" vs "550 days":** Both figures appear in authoritative sources. Either is defensible; cite Access Now for 552 or HRW for 550. Do not round to "nearly 600 days" — that overstates it.

3. **Manipur emergency impact:** The peer-reviewed study documents healthcare system harm (delays in patient care, consultant access), not a specific documented fatality. The Kashmir cases (HRW 2019) are stronger for the "emergency access denied" narrative.

4. **Disaster + shutdown overlap:** The co-occurrence is documented but no single authoritative report has explicitly written "shutdown X prevented response to disaster Y" in Manipur/NE India with case-study rigor. The Kashmir 2019 cases (ambulance, miscarriage, COVID-19 outreach) are the most citable.
