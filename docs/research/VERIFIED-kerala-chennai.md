# Fact-Check: Kerala 2018 Floods + Chennai 2015 Floods
**Verified by:** Fact-checker agent (independent source re-fetch)
**Date:** 2026-07-08

## KERALA FLOODS, AUGUST 2018

### Claim 1: "Worst floods in ~100 years"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**
**Exact supportable wording:** "worst flooding and landslides in 100 years" / "worst flood in nearly a century"
**Best canonical source:** [ARRL Kerala Flooding News](http://www.arrl.org/news/view/kerala-india-flooding-radio-amateurs-assist-rescue-operations)
**Direct quote:** "Kerala was hit by the worst flooding and landslides in 100 years" (ARRL); Chief Minister Pinarayi Vijayan stated "the state is facing its worst flood in 100 years."
**Confidence: High**
**Notes:** Multiple official and major media sources corroborate this. Kerala received 457% more rain than usual during the 2018 monsoon.

---

### Claim 2: "5.4 million displaced"
**Verdict: UNVERIFIED**
**Exact supportable figure:** ~1 million people evacuated/displaced is the figure most consistently cited by official sources. The 5.4 million figure was not found in any primary source reviewed.
**Best canonical source:** [Wikipedia – 2018 Kerala Floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Kerala_floods) cites "about one million people were evacuated." At peak (21 August), 1.45 million were in relief camps.
**Direct quote:** "about one million people were evacuated" (Wikipedia, citing National Disaster Management Authority data)
**Confidence: High (that 5.4M is NOT the right figure)**
**Notes:** The figure 5.4 million displaced does not appear in NDMA, UNDP PDNA, or major news sources. The 1.24 million in relief camps is a different (higher-precision) figure. "Displaced" could be interpreted broadly to include all who left homes temporarily, but no authoritative source gives 5.4M. Recommend not using this figure without a named source.

---

### Claim 3: "1.24 million people in 3,274 relief camps"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**
**Exact supportable wording:** 1,247,496 people in 3,274+ relief camps (peak figure)
**Best canonical source:** [Wikipedia – 2018 Kerala Floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Kerala_floods) citing National Disaster Management Authority
**Direct quote:** "Over 3,274 relief camps were opened … 1,247,496 people found shelter in such camps" (NDMA data via Wikipedia)
**Confidence: High**
**Notes:** Peak was ~1.45 million in over 3,300 camps (21 August per some sources). The 1,247,496 / 3,274 figure represents a specific point-in-time count, likely slightly before peak. Both figures are documented.

---

### Claim 4: "483 dead" (official final toll)
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (with range caveat)**
**Exact supportable figure:** 483 deaths is the widely-cited official figure; some sources cite slightly over 500 total (including post-flood disease deaths)
**Best canonical source:** Multiple including [FloodList](https://floodlist.com/asia/india-kerala-floods-update2-august-2018) and Wikipedia infobox
**Direct quote:** "Over 483 people died" (Wikipedia/Indian Express); ARRL article cited "over 300 deaths" at an earlier reporting date.
**Confidence: High**
**Notes:** 483 is the standard cited official toll for direct flood deaths. Some final assessments cite closer to 500 when including indirect deaths. "483" is defensible as a specific cited figure.

---

### Claim 5: "All 14 districts faced a communications breakdown"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (with precision caveat)**
**Exact supportable wording:** All 14 districts were placed on red alert and roads/communications were non-functional in worst-affected districts; some less-affected districts had partial communications.
**Best canonical source:** [Wikipedia – 2018 Kerala Floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Kerala_floods); [NDRF Kerala 2018](https://ndrf.gov.in/en/operations/kerala-floods-2018)
**Direct quote:** "roads were submerged and no communication system was functional in the worst affected districts"
**Confidence: Medium**
**Notes:** 13 of 14 districts saw severe flooding; all 14 were on red alert. "All 14 districts faced a communications breakdown" is slightly overstated — the breakdown was concentrated in the most severely flooded districts (Idukki, Alappuzha, Pathanamthitta, Wayanad, Ernakulam, Thrissur, Kottayam). A more defensible phrasing is "communications failed across most of the state's 14 districts."

---

### Claim 6: Root cause of network failure was power/diesel/battery exhaustion (NOT tower destruction)
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**
**Exact supportable wording:** Primary failure mode was power outage → battery depletion → diesel generator dependency → diesel supply disruption. Physical tower destruction was a secondary/minor factor.
**Best canonical source:** [Scroll.in – Kerala mobile networks breakdown](https://scroll.in/latest/890953/parts-of-kerala-face-mobile-network-breakdown-as-floods-disrupt-electricity-and-fuel-supply); [TelecomLead – Kerala floods mobile operators](https://www.telecomlead.com/telecom-services/kerala-floods-mobile-operators-fail-to-connect-during-disaster-85906)
**Direct quote:** "Most of the phone subscribers lost connectivity in the past several days because mobile operators did not receive power or alternative fuel resources to support their telecom infrastructure in the state." More than 2,500 towers "operated on diesel generator support due to power outage" — the failure came from diesel supply disruption, not tower destruction.
**Confidence: High**

### Claim 7: "Chalakudy town: all five operators (Jio, BSNL, Vodafone, Airtel, Reliance) failed simultaneously; couldn't send an SMS"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (with source note)**
**Exact supportable wording:** Chalakudy experienced complete connectivity loss with all major operators failing; this is documented in telecom-sector news coverage.
**Best canonical source:** [TelecomLead](https://www.telecomlead.com/telecom-services/kerala-floods-mobile-operators-fail-to-connect-during-disaster-85906); confirmed by search result summary citing the five operators specifically.
**Direct quote:** "Chalakudy town [experienced] absolutely no connectivity where people couldn't even send an SMS, and all five operators—Jio, Reliance, BSNL, Vodafone, and Airtel—failed to provide service."
**Confidence: Medium-High**
**Notes:** The Chalakudy specific detail appears in the TelecomLead article. The article was not fully fetchable to extract a verbatim quote — the claim came through search result snippet. Confidence is medium-high rather than high because a direct verbatim quote from the original article couldn't be extracted due to fetch limitations. The claim is directionally solid but the exact source article should be verified by a human.

### Claim 8: HAM Radio — ~300 (or 120–300) operators at Thiruvananthapuram District Administration office located 15,000+ stranded people and facilitated 1,800+ rescues (1,650 in Aug 16–19). Operator: Suwil Wilson VU2IT?
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (with precise figures from ARRL)**
**Exact supportable figures:**
- Operators: "some 120 hams — and perhaps as many as 300" (ARRL)
- Stranded people located: "more than 15,000 victims stranded on roofs of houses and other buildings" (ARRL)
- Rescues: "over 1,800 people rescued" total; "1,650 people rescued … from the Thiruvananthapuram centre between August 16 and August 19" (ARRL)
- Key operator: Suwil Wilson, VU2IT — CONFIRMED by ARRL as coordinating statewide response
- Also named: Ram Mohan Suri, VU2MY (director, National Institute of Amateur Radio)
**Best canonical source:** [ARRL – Kerala India Flooding Radio Amateurs](http://www.arrl.org/news/view/kerala-india-flooding-radio-amateurs-assist-rescue-operations)
**Direct quote:** "Some 120 hams — and perhaps as many as 300 — worked around the clock … Hams have reported the location and other details of more than 15,000 victims stranded on roofs of houses … 1,650 people [rescued] from the Thiruvananthapuram centre between August 16 and August 19" (ARRL)
**Confidence: High**
**Notes:** The total "1,800+" figure is from combining the Thiruvananthapuram centre (1,650) plus operations from other centres. The ARRL article is the primary authoritative source and directly confirms all key sub-claims including VU2IT.

### Claim 9: "Fishermen rescued 65,000+ people using ~669 private boats"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (with labelling caveat)**
**Exact supportable wording:** 4,537 fishermen using 669 mechanised fishing boats rescued 65,000+ people. These were not "private boats" in the generic sense — they were fishing community boats from Kollam and Thiruvananthapuram districts.
**Best canonical source:** [Onmanorama – Kerala fishermen rescued 65,000](https://www.onmanorama.com/news/kerala/2018/08/26/kerala-flood-fishermen-rescue.html); Wikipedia citing government fisheries records.
**Direct quote:** "Fishermen in 669 mechanised country boats rescued a total of 65,000 people marooned in all districts. A total of 4,537 fishermen communities from Kollam and Thiruvananthapuram districts participated."
**Confidence: High**
**Notes:** Some sources round up to 70,000+ rescued by fishermen. The 65,000 / 669 boats / 4,537 fishermen figures are consistent across sources. Use "fishing boats" rather than "private boats" for accuracy.

### Claim 10: Migrant workers were "last priority" in rescue; non-Malayalee workers missed Malayalam-only warnings; excluded from leptospirosis prophylaxis and loss assessments
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (documented in peer-reviewed literature and UN-commissioned study)**
**Exact supportable wording:** Migrants "were discriminated in rescue, relief and rehabilitation"; "missed the alerts in local languages resulting in poor preparedness"; "missed out in the drug administration to prevent spike of cases of leptospirosis"; "damage and loss assessment also was not inclusive"
**Best canonical source:** [PMC – Inclusion of Interstate Migrant Workers in Kerala](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7659401/) citing Peter and Narendran (2019) — a study commissioned by UN with the Government of Kerala
**Direct quote:** "The study found out that the migrants were discriminated in rescue, relief and rehabilitation … They had missed the alerts in local languages resulting in poor preparedness … They also were missed out in the drug administration to prevent spike of cases of leptospirosis after the flood."
**Confidence: High**
**Notes:** "Last priority" is an editorial characterisation rather than an exact quote, but it accurately captures the documented discrimination. The underlying facts are solidly documented.

### Claim 11: "Fake 'dam collapsed' news spread in migrant WhatsApp groups causing chaos"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (partially — fake dam news confirmed; "migrant WhatsApp groups specifically" is harder to verify)**
**Exact supportable wording:** Fake audio/messages claiming dams had burst or were collapsing spread on WhatsApp widely and caused panic, hindering rescue operations. Attribution specifically to migrant WhatsApp groups is plausible but not explicitly documented in sources reviewed.
**Best canonical source:** [BOOM Live – Kerala Floods Fake News Dam Burst](https://www.boomlive.in/kerala-floods-fake-news-about-dam-burst-no-power-create-panic); [The News Minute – Kerala floods fake news](https://www.thenewsminute.com/kerala/kerala-floods-govt-warns-strict-action-against-those-spreading-fake-news-old-videos-106929)
**Direct quote:** "An audio recording falsely claiming that the Mullaperiyar dam was leaking and would soon collapse … went viral and caused immense panic." Government warned of legal action under Disaster Management Act.
**Confidence: Medium**
**Notes:** The fake dam-collapse news on WhatsApp is confirmed. The specific sub-claim that it "spread in migrant WhatsApp groups" targeting non-Malayalee migrant workers is plausible (migrants would be more susceptible given language barriers and unfamiliarity with geography), but this exact formulation was not found in a named source. Mark "migrant-specific targeting" as UNVERIFIED at source level.

### Claim 12: Kerala Rescue app was built by student volunteers and adopted by the government
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**
**Exact supportable wording:** Kerala Rescue was initially built by student/tech volunteers (started as a Google spreadsheet, evolved into a platform by college students and then 200+ developers) and was adopted/endorsed by the Kerala state government.
**Best canonical source:** [Wikipedia – 2018 Kerala Floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Kerala_floods); [NPR – Social media came to the rescue](https://www.npr.org/sections/goatsandsoda/2018/08/22/640879582/how-social-media-came-to-the-rescue-after-keralas-flood)
**Direct quote:** "Kerala Rescue is an emergency request platform … created by a college student from Kerala during the early stages of the flood … quickly becoming a widely used service supported by Kerala's state government … an additional 200 software developers were actively developing the site."
**Confidence: High**

## CHENNAI FLOODS, DECEMBER 2015

### Claim 13: City cut off for over a week; ~3,500 stranded at airport
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**
**Exact supportable wording:** City effectively cut off; Chennai airport closed (flooded runway); over 3,500 people stranded at the airport.
**Best canonical source:** [ATPI – Chennai floods disaster management](https://www.atpi.com/case/chennai-floods-disaster-management/); [Wikipedia – 2015 South India Floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_South_India_floods)
**Direct quote:** "The capital, Chennai, ground to a complete halt with over 3,500 people stranded at Chennai airport alone. The runway at Chennai airport was flooded and got closed for 3 days."
**Confidence: Medium-High**
**Notes:** Airport closure is confirmed (Wikipedia says "closed until 6 December"). The "3,500 stranded at airport" figure appears in multiple sources. The "over a week" framing for the city being cut off is broadly supported though the exact duration of "complete" isolation varies by source. Wikipedia notes flooding continued for approximately two weeks.

### Claim 14: Network failed from combination of tower damage + power outage + fuel shortage + BTS flooding + congestion
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (partially — power outage, fuel shortage, congestion confirmed; tower damage and BTS flooding confirmed in principle but less granularly documented)**
**Exact supportable wording:** Network failures resulted primarily from widespread power outages (60% of the city lost power), inability to charge devices, and network congestion. Power outages at sub-stations along the Adyar River caused week-long outages. Physical tower/BTS damage by flooding is documented in the broader literature on telecom disaster response but less specifically cited for Chennai 2015 than for Kerala 2018.
**Best canonical source:** [Wikipedia – 2015 South India Floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_South_India_floods); [The Federal – How Chennai managed power supply](https://thefederal.com/states/south/tamil-nadu/how-flooded-chennai-managed-to-keep-power-supply-almost-uninterrupted/)
**Direct quote:** "power supplies were suspended to 60% of the city"; "no way to charge their cellphones"; "Guindy sub-station and sub-stations along the river or even close to the sea were totally submerged, cutting off power supply for a week."
**Confidence: Medium**
**Notes:** Power outage → battery depletion → network failure chain is well-documented. "Tower damage" and "BTS flooding" as distinct causes are implied by infrastructure flooding but not as granularly sourced as in Kerala 2018. The claim as a whole (multi-factor failure) is defensible. Don't claim equal evidential weight for each factor.

### Claim 15: HAM RADIO — Indian Institute of Hams activated a 7.070 MHz emergency net / repeaters as first responders
**Verdict: CONFIRMED (frequency and net confirmed; "first responders" label is not in the source)**
**Exact supportable wording:** A net was established on 7.070 MHz during Chennai 2015 floods; repeaters were also activated. The Amateur Radio Society of India (ARSI) coordinated the response; the Indian Institute of Hams (IIH) set up a station. "First responders" is not how the ARRL article describes them.
**Best canonical source:** [ARRL – Radio Amateurs Respond to Grim Flood Situation in Southern India](https://www.arrl.org/news/radio-amateurs-respond-to-grim-flood-situation-in-southern-india)
**Direct quote:** "A net was established on 7.070 MHz, and repeaters were also operational." Lead named: Gopal Madhavan (VU2GMN), ARSI president.
**Confidence: Medium-High**
**Notes:** The 7.070 MHz frequency and IIH/ARSI involvement are confirmed by ARRL. The response was hampered by power outages (batteries discharged), flooded roads, and impassable bridges — it was a supporting/coordination role, not explicitly "first responders." Drop that label or add a caveat.

## CORRECTIONS / FLAGS FOR TEAM-LEAD

1. **Claim 2 (5.4M displaced):** COULD NOT VERIFY. Official sources consistently cite ~1M evacuated / 1.24M in camps. The 5.4M figure did not appear in NDMA, UNDP, or major news sources. **Do not use without a named primary source.**

2. **Claim 9 (669 "private boats"):** Minor labelling issue. These were fishing community boats — "669 fishing boats" is more accurate than "private boats."

3. **Claim 11 (migrant WhatsApp groups specifically):** The fake dam news on WhatsApp is confirmed broadly; the targeting of migrant-specific WhatsApp groups is plausible but was not found explicitly stated in a named source. Either drop the "migrant groups" qualifier or source it to the Peter & Narendran study directly.

4. **Claim 15 (HAM as "first responders"):** ARRL does not use this label. The Chennai HAM response was constrained by power outages and flooded roads. "Emergency communicators" or "emergency net operators" is what the sources actually support.

5. **Claim 5 (all 14 districts):** Strictly, all 14 were on red alert but "all 14 faced communications breakdown" overstates slightly — the most severe breakdown was in ~8–10 worst-hit districts. This is a minor precision issue.
