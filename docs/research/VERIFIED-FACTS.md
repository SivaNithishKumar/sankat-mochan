## ✅ THE ANCHOR — Kerala 2018 ham radio (your strongest, fully verified)

**CONFIRMED (high).** During the 2018 Kerala floods, **120–300 volunteer amateur-radio operators**
assembled at the Thiruvananthapuram District Administration office and, by tracing victims' last
mobile-phone locations, **located 15,000+ stranded people and facilitated 1,800+ rescues** — with
**1,650 rescues from that one centre between Aug 16–19**. Coordinator: **Suwil Wilson (VU2IT)**.
- Pitch use: *"In 2018, volunteers with radios did by hand exactly what we're systematizing — and we add the AI layer so the migrant workers who were rescued last aren't missed."*
- Source: ARRL — http://www.arrl.org/news/kerala-india-flooding-radio-amateurs-assist-rescue-operations

## ✅ THE STRONGEST WARNING-FAILURE STORY — Wayanad Hume Centre (fully verified, RTI-backed)

**CONFIRMED (high).** The **Hume Centre for Ecology & Wildlife Biology** sent a landslide warning at
**~9 AM on 29 July 2024 via WhatsApp — ~16 hours before** the disaster. The district Collectorate's
own **RTI response (Oct 2024)** denied receiving it and stated Hume Centre warnings "are meant to be
discouraged" because it lacks nodal-agency status. **Damning corroboration:** in **2020 the same
Collectorate *did* act on a Hume Centre WhatsApp alert and saved lives in Mundakkai** — making the
2024 inaction worse. Over 420 died.
- Source: Onmanorama lessons-learnt + the Collectorate RTI response (Oct 2024).

## ✅ Verified event facts (safe to cite)

### Kerala floods, Aug 2018
- Worst floods in ~100 years. **CONFIRMED.**
- **483 dead** (some sources ~500 incl. indirect). **CONFIRMED.**
- **1.24M in 3,274 relief camps**; ~1M evacuated. **CONFIRMED.**
- Network failure root cause = **power outage → battery drain → generator diesel exhaustion** (not tower destruction). **CONFIRMED.** Chalakudy: all five operators down, "couldn't send an SMS." (Medium-high.)
- **Fishing community rescued 65,000+** using 669 mechanised boats / 4,537 fishermen. **CONFIRMED.**
- **Migrant workers deprioritized in rescue, missed Malayalam-only warnings, excluded from leptospirosis prophylaxis + loss assessments.** **CONFIRMED** (Peter & Narendran 2019, PMC).
- Kerala Rescue app: student-volunteer built, scaled to 200+ devs, state-adopted. **CONFIRMED.**

### Chennai floods, Dec 2015
- City cut off ~a week; ~3,500 stranded at the flooded airport. **CONFIRMED (med-high).**
- Network failure multi-factor: power loss (~60% of city), device-charging failure, congestion (tower/BTS damage implied). **CONFIRMED (partial).**
- IIH/ARSI (Gopal Madhavan VU2GMN) activated a **7.070 MHz emergency net + repeaters**. **CONFIRMED.**
- Citizens built **chennairains.org** (from a Google sheet by Sowmya Rao) — used by police/officials. **CONFIRMED.**

### Kedarnath / Uttarakhand, June 2013
- **~300,000 trapped.** **CONFIRMED.**
- **580 confirmed dead; ~4,120–5,748 missing (presumed dead); ~6,054 total.** **CONFIRMED** (note: two time-points).
- Telecom at Kedarnath/Dharchula/Narayan Bagar **washed away**; BSNL restored **272 towers + 44 exchanges.** **CONFIRMED.**
- **Rudraprayag: no district magistrate for 4 days.** **CONFIRMED.**
- **Government imported 25 satellite phones from Hong Kong** mid-operation. **CONFIRMED — but attribute to India Today (28 June 2013), not as a government-confirmed fact.**
- Operation Surya Hope: **108,653 evacuated**, 83 aircraft, 2,137 IAF sorties, ITBP rescued 33,009. **CONFIRMED.**
- Stranded pilgrims relayed ~50 messages (names/numbers) via soldiers when networks were down. **PARTIAL — hedge the wording.**

### Wayanad landslides, 30 July 2024
- **Over 420 dead** (403–500 across sources); 118 missing. **CONFIRMED.**
- Unidentifiable bodies → **431 autopsy + 172 blood DNA samples; 31 bodies + 158 body parts in numbered graves** at Puthumala. **CONFIRMED.**
- Bridge over Punnapuzha (at Chooralmala) destroyed, isolating ~400 families; **190-ft Bailey bridge built in 31 hours** (144 Madras Engineers). **CONFIRMED.**
- **BSNL 4G restored by afternoon of 31 July** (~24h) via diesel towers; Jio added a tower; Airtel free data. **CONFIRMED** — real chokepoint was **road/bridge access**, not the network.
- Victims mainly tea/cardamom **plantation & inter-state migrant workers**, limited literacy, warnings didn't reach them. **CONFIRMED.**

### Sikkim GLOF (South Lhonak), Oct 2023
- **55 dead / 75 missing by Oct 6; 92 confirmed dead by Oct 18.** **CONFIRMED.**
- Teesta III dam destroyed; **NH-10 collapsed, north Sikkim cut off.** **CONFIRMED.**
- **No functioning Early Warning System at the lake** despite GLOF risk flagged since 2005 (a camera system installed Sept 2023 was non-functional as a warning system). **CONFIRMED.**
- Airtel fibre "major breakdown," repaired via IG Drones survey. **CONFIRMED.**
- Reframe the dam point per DO-NOT-USE table (spillway designed for rain floods only, GLOF excluded).

### Himachal Pradesh monsoon, 2023
- **424–500 dead** (424 DD News, ~500 NDMA PDNA); **5,748 landslides; 1,000+ roads blocked.** **CONFIRMED.**
- DoT activated **Intra-Circle Roaming (ICR)**. **CONFIRMED.**
- **Only 31 weather stations operational** in the whole state (NDMA PDNA). **CONFIRMED.**
- DDMAs lacked protocols / comms infrastructure / trained personnel; **"fragmented inter-agency coordination"** (use this wording, not "duplication of relief effort" — that phrase is unsourced). **CONFIRMED (partial).**

### Joshimath subsidence, Jan 2023
- ISRO/NRSC report (**5.4 cm subsidence in 12 days**) published then **removed on 13 Jan 2023**; same day NDMA issued an Office Memorandum to ~12 research bodies to stop sharing data publicly. **CONFIRMED** (The Wire, Business Today, Outlook, Tribune).
- **278 families (~900 people)** displaced to relief camps. **CONFIRMED.**

## ✅ Standing pitch-backbone facts (verified, reusable)

**NDMA's own satellite network — the apex-authority backstop.** **CONFIRMED (load-bearing).**
Official program name is **"National Disaster Management Services (NDMS)"** — covers **81 vulnerable
districts + 36 state/UT HQs + national HQs = 120 locations**, each with a fixed VSAT terminal + an
ISAT satellite phone. Official rationale, verbatim: **"The terrestrial communication network is prone
to failure during disaster."** Completed 31 March 2019.
- Source: ndma.gov.in (IT & Comm Projects); msdma.gov.in/ndms.html
- Pitch use: pre-empts "does the network really fail?" — India's apex disaster body says yes, in policy.

**Satellite messengers are illegal in India.** **CONFIRMED (load-bearing).** Under the Wireless
Telegraphy Act 1933 (S.6) + Indian Telegraph Act (S.20), Garmin inReach / SPOT / Thuraya / Iridium
are banned without a licence. **Documented arrests:** a Scottish trekker detained at Delhi airport;
a Canadian ultra-runner arrested at Goa (bail + passport confiscated); **March 2025, Delhi IGI — 3
Americans + 1 Australian booked** under telecom laws. Fines ₹50K–₹1L+.
- Source: ExplorersWeb.
- Pitch use: there is no legal, affordable off-grid alternative for a civilian in India.

**Starlink is not commercially available in India.** **CONFIRMED.** Has IN-SPACe approval, but India
**froze final commercial clearance in June 2026** over security concerns. Announced pricing: **₹8,600/mo
+ ₹34,000 hardware.** Source: SpaceNews; Convergence Now.

**~60 million inter-state migrants in India.** **CONFIRMED.** Economic Survey 2016-17 estimate
(Census 2011 direct count: 41.4M). Cite with the vintage. Source: PRS India.
- Pitch use: grounds the multilingual-triage value story.

**Internet shutdowns (only if ever relevant — angle is dropped):** India is **#2 globally** — 84 in
2024, 65 in 2025 (Access Now). Say "most of any democracy," never "most in the world."

## ✅ MYANMAR earthquake, 28 March 2025 (verified — use with framing care)

**Optional showcase.** The strongest single case of total comms collapse + delayed help. All 14
checked claims are CONFIRMED or mostly-confirmed (none FALSE). **Framing rule:** lead with the
*humanitarian impact*, use the numbers as *evidence*, never as a political accusation against the
junta. Cite Context/TRF, Access Now, UN OCHA — not opposition media — for contested figures.

**Load-bearing, low-sensitivity, high pitch-utility (use these):**
- **"Three days passed before help reached one town"** — due to comms blackout. **CONFIRMED.** Cite: humanitarian worker quoted by **Context/Thomson Reuters Foundation, 9 Apr 2025**.
- **"Residents in blackout zones had to travel for several days to reach connectivity to call in their needs."** **CONFIRMED**, same Context/TRF source. *This is your cleanest "our app would have helped" line.*
- Internet cost: **~$0.48 for a 2-hour session vs ~$2.20 average daily wage**; Starlink terminal ~$389. **CONFIRMED** (Context/TRF).
- **~9 million severely affected; ~17 million total.** **CONFIRMED** (UN OCHA).
- Self-rescue: Mandalay Fire Services rescued **403 and recovered 259 bodies by 31 March.** **CONFIRMED.**

**Confirmed but politically loaded (handle carefully; lead humanitarian, not political):**
- Sagaing (epicenter) was **already under a military internet blackout before the quake**; junta shutdowns covered 100+ townships (~⅓ of the country). **CONFIRMED (high).**
- Starlink is **banned in Myanmar**, and **users reportedly risked military confiscation** of terminals. Ban = CONFIRMED; confiscation = **reported/alleged** ("we heard that…" — Htaike Htaike Aung, Rest of World). Say "risked confiscation," not "were confiscated."
- Myanmar had **~85 internet shutdowns in 2024 — most of any country that year.** **CONFIRMED** (Access Now 2024 report).
- Quote (verified, verbatim): **"Internet shutdowns are a matter of life and death during this time of crisis."** — Htaike Htaike Aung, Rest of World, 3 Apr 2025.
- Post-quake airstrikes + selective aid obstruction to resistance areas: CONFIRMED but **specify the window** ("dozens documented," not "72+" as a total). Most politically sensitive — probably skip in a product pitch.

**Basics / casualties (CONFIRMED, note the disparities):**
- Mw **7.7** (7.7–7.9 defensible), ~500 km rupture, 6.7 aftershock at +12 min, **12:50 PM during Friday prayers.**
- Deaths: **~5,352 (independent/Mizzima)** vs **~3,600 (junta)**; injured ~7,100–11,366; ~538 missing. Use the disparity honestly if quoting.
- Mosque deaths: use **"up to 700"** or "hundreds" (contested 250–700+). Condo collapse 207, bank 40, ~80 monks — CONFIRMED.

**Honest call on whether to use Myanmar:** it's the strongest "comms totally gone, help delayed days" showcase you have — but it's foreign and politically sensitive, at an India-grounded hackathon. If you use it, use ONLY the low-sensitivity humanitarian lines (3-day delay, travel-days-for-a-call, cost) as a *brief* contrast, then pivot straight back to the Indian cases. Don't build the pitch on it.
