## KEDARNATH / UTTARAKHAND FLOODS, JUNE 2013

---

### Claim 1: "~300,000 people trapped"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

Wikipedia (2013 North India floods) confirms: "approximately 300,000 pilgrims and tourists became trapped in valleys leading to Hindu pilgrimage sites." By June 18 alone, more than 12,000 were stranded at Badrinath. The 300,000 figure is the widely cited and uncontested trapped-persons count.

**Best source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_North_India_floods
**Confidence: High**

---

### Claim 2: "580 confirmed dead; thousands missing (4,120–5,748)"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED WITH NUANCE — figures are real but represent different points in time**

- **580 confirmed dead** was the official figure *as of mid-July 2013*, before missing persons were reclassified as presumed dead.
- **5,748 missing** was the figure as of July 16, 2013 (924 local residents + 4,824 from other states).
- **4,120 missing** reflects a later revised downward estimate in September 2013 by Chief Secretary Subhash Kumar (including 421 children).
- The NIDM 2014 report gives 169 confirmed dead + 4,021 missing (presumed dead).
- The total deaths *including presumed dead* is commonly cited as ~6,000; the Wikipedia article states **6,054 total deaths**.
- NDMA's own vice-chairman Shashidhar Reddy estimated "more than 10,000" killed as early as July 1 — this early estimate proved inflated.

The 580/4,120–5,748 figures are accurate but need the temporal caveat: 580 confirmed dead at the time, with 4,120–5,748 missing/presumed dead.

**Best source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_North_India_floods; https://factly.in/the-death-toll-of-2013-uttarakhand-floods-stands-around-5000-not-25000/
**Confidence: High**

### Claim 3 (KEY): "The government imported 25 satellite phones from Hong Kong mid-operation for NDMA/NDRF"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED — but sourced to a single 2013 India Today piece, not a government document**

Wikipedia's Operation Surya Hope article contains the verbatim text:

> "Government imports 25 satellite phones from Hong Kong for the ill-prepared NDMA and NDRF."

This is dated **26 June 2013** in the timeline. Wikipedia's cited source is:

> Reference [35]: Bhavna Vij-Aurora; Amarnath K. Menon (28 June 2013). "Lessons still Unlearned: Another national calamity, another instance of apathy and disaster mismanagement by unprepared state and central governments" — *India Today*.

**Assessment:** The claim is real and sourced to contemporaneous India Today reporting (not a government document). The framing — "ill-prepared NDMA and NDRF" — is from the India Today article's characterisation. The fact of importation is credible journalism from 2013; no government confirmation or denial found. **Use with attribution to India Today (June 2013), not as an independently confirmed government fact.** No other primary source corroborates the specific number "25" or "Hong Kong."

**Best source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Surya_Hope (citing India Today, 28 June 2013)
**Direct quote:** "Government imports 25 satellite phones from Hong Kong for the ill-prepared NDMA and NDRF."
**Confidence: Medium** (single journalistic source; plausible but unverified by a second independent source)

### Claim 4: "Telecom at Kedarnath/Dharchula/Narayan Bagar was washed away; BSNL later restored ~272 towers + 44 exchanges"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

Telecomlead.com and Business Standard both report: BSNL restored **272 mobile towers** and **44 telephone exchanges** in Uttarakhand. The three locations where equipment was completely washed away and could NOT be restored were: **Dharchula, Narayan Bazar, and Kedarnath**. This matches the claim precisely.

**Best source:** https://www.telecomlead.com/telecom-services/telecom-operators-gear-up-to-restore-operations-in-flood-hit-uttarakhand-74168-32341
**Confidence: High**

---

### Claim 5: "Rudraprayag had no district magistrate for 4 days"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

Multiple sources confirm: Rudraprayag district had no district magistrate for four days following the disaster. Additionally, no deputy collector was posted in Gaurikund, Sonprayag, Phata, and Guptkashi for five days. Both the administrative vacuum and its duration are corroborated.

**Best source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_North_India_floods; search results citing the Uttarakhand flash floods ReliefWeb report.
**Confidence: High**

### Claim 6: "Home Minister Sushil Kumar Shinde admitted 'no coordination among government agencies'"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED IN SUBSTANCE — exact wording unverified**

Multiple sources confirm that Home Minister Sushil Kumar Shinde visited Uttarakhand and publicly admitted there was "no coordination among government agencies," which was hampering rescue operations. The Down to Earth article (heavens-rage-41497) corroborates this. The exact wording of Shinde's statement varies by source; it is a real admission, not fabricated.

**Best source:** Down to Earth, June 2013 reporting; ReliefWeb Uttarakhand flash floods report.
**Confidence: Medium-High** (substance confirmed; exact quote wording not independently verified from Shinde's original statement)

### Claim 7: "Stranded people handed soldiers physical lists of names/phone numbers to deliver to families (networks down)"
**Verdict: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — the practice occurred but the specific "physical lists" framing has limited primary sourcing**

The Army ReliefWeb dispatch ("Army Connects with People in Kedarnath and Badrinath Vallies" — URL access returned 403) is the primary source. Search results indicate that around 50 messages were delivered to families of stranded people through this mechanism — soldiers collected contact details and relayed messages. The practice is corroborated by multiple accounts of communication collapse and army acting as messengers. However, the specific phrase "physical lists handed to soldiers" is not verified verbatim from a primary government report.

**Assessment:** The core fact — that stranded pilgrims gave contact information to soldiers who physically relayed it — is credible and multiply sourced. The "physical lists" framing is a reasonable inference from contemporary accounts of network collapse, but the exact format is not confirmed in a primary source.

**Confidence: Medium**

### Claim 8: "Operation Surya Hope: ~110,000+ evacuated; helicopters dominant (83 aircraft, ~2,137 sorties); ITBP rescued ~33,009"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED — with precision corrections**

- **Total evacuations:** Wikipedia states **108,653** people evacuated by air and foot by July 15 (not "110,000+", though this rounds close). The "110,000" figure cited elsewhere refers to IAF alone in some contexts; total is 108,653.
- **Aircraft:** 83 total by June 23 (IAF-45, Army-13, civil-25). CONFIRMED.
- **IAF sorties:** 2,137 sorties from June 17–30. CONFIRMED.
- **ITBP:** ITBP rescued **33,009** pilgrims in 15 days from remote areas. CONFIRMED. (Note: ITBP deployment strength was 600 as of June 20; the 33,009 rescued figure is a cumulative operation total.)
- For completeness: IAF rescued 23,775; Army 38,750; NDRF 9,000.

**Best source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Surya_Hope
**Confidence: High**

### Claim 9: "NDMA 'failed miserably in its first major challenge'" — find who said/wrote this
**Verdict: UNVERIFIED — phrase is real but attribution to a specific speaker/writer not confirmed**

The phrase "failed miserably in its first major challenge" appears in commentary about the 2013 disaster and is associated with criticism of NDMA's performance. The CAG (Comptroller and Auditor General) documented that NDMA had not performed its prescribed functions. India Today's June 2013 coverage ("Lessons still Unlearned") characterised NDMA as "ill-prepared." However, the specific phrase "failed miserably in its first major challenge" — as an attributable quote — cannot be pinned to a named individual or specific report from the sources available.

**Assessment:** The *sentiment* is corroborated by multiple credible sources (India Today, Down to Earth, CAG report). The exact phrase may be a journalistic characterisation rather than a direct quote from a named official. **Do not use as a direct quotation without finding the India Today or CAG report text.**

**Confidence: Low for specific attribution; Medium for the substance of the criticism**

## WAYANAD LANDSLIDES, 30 JULY 2024

---

### Claim 10: Death toll — "254 confirmed" vs. revised counts (420?)
**Verdict: CORRECTED — 254 was an early figure; the defensible final official count is ~420**

The death toll evolved:
- Early reports: 249–254 confirmed dead
- Wikipedia (2024 Wayanad landslides): **"over 420 killed"**
- Down to Earth (one month after): "over 231 confirmed dead + body parts of 218 others recovered" (reflecting ongoing identification)
- The Bailey bridge search result stated "over 420 killed and 397 injured"
- PUCL and other human rights reports cite 403 official deaths, with locals believing 500+ given ~100 still unaccounted for

**Best defensible figure:** ~420 confirmed/presumed dead (official Kerala government figure). The 254 figure was a mid-operation count from approximately July 31, not the final figure.

**Best source:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Wayanad_landslides
**Confidence: High** (for ~420; the exact final figure varies 403–420 across sources)

---

### Claim 11: "118 missing; DNA identification of unidentifiable bodies; numbered graves"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED WITH PRECISION**

- **118 missing:** Cited in multiple sources alongside the 254 early death toll; later revised down as identification progressed.
- **DNA identification:** CONFIRMED. Kerala used next-generation DNA sequencing; DNA profiles created from **431 autopsy samples** and **172 blood samples** at Kannur Regional Forensic Lab. Over months, unidentified victims reduced from 138 to 32.
- **Numbered graves:** CONFIRMED. In Puthumala, **31 unclaimed bodies and over 158 body parts** were buried after interfaith prayers. Each grave was marked with a DNA sample number on a small board; a plastic bottle with the identification number was also buried with the body.

**Best sources:** https://www.deccanherald.com/india/kerala/wayanad-landslide-31-unclaimed-bodies-and-158-body-parts-to-be-buried-3137305; https://www.onmanorama.com/news/kerala/2024/08/06/kerala-wayanad-landslide-victims-dna-profile.html
**Confidence: High**

---

### Claim 12 (KEY): "Hume Centre issued warning ~16 hours before; district administration didn't act because it 'wasn't integrated with the official warning system'"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED IN SUBSTANCE — with important nuance on timing and the Collectorate's counter-claim**

**What is verified:**
- The Hume Centre for Ecology and Wildlife Biology (Kalpetta) operates 200+ meteorological stations in Wayanad.
- Hume Centre issued a landslide alert at **9 a.m. on July 29**, 16 hours before the landslides struck in the early hours of July 30. Alert was based on: Puthumala station recorded 200mm rainfall followed by 130mm overnight (600mm threshold for landslide risk).
- The district administration issued its own warning **10:35 p.m. on July 29** (14 hours after the Hume Centre alert), but **did not mention evacuation**.
- The Wayanad Collectorate's RTI response (October 26, 2024) **emphatically denied** receiving the Hume Centre's warning, calling it "not official information."
- The administration's formal position: "Hume Centre for Ecology and Wildlife Biology is not one among the nodal agencies identified for issuing disaster alerts" — warnings from it "are meant to be discouraged."
- The Hume Centre director stated he personally called officials urging evacuation.
- **Irony confirmed:** In 2020, the same Wayanad administration acted on a Hume Centre WhatsApp alert and relocated people from Mundakkai, saving lives.

**Assessment:** The "wasn't integrated with the official warning system" framing is accurate and supported by multiple primary sources. The Collectorate's denial makes this even more damning — the official position is that Hume Centre warnings *should not be acted on* because the Centre lacks nodal status, despite demonstrated accuracy.

**Best sources:** https://www.onmanorama.com/news/kerala/2024/10/27/wayanad-collectorate-emphatically-rejects-hume-centre-claimit-provided-landslide-alert.html; https://www.onmanorama.com/news/kerala/2024/11/28/hume-centre-warnings-illegitimate-meant-discouraged-wayanad-collectorate.html; https://www.downtoearth.org.in/natural-disasters/wayanads-hume-centre-for-ecology-wildlife-which-warned-of-landslides-urges-separate-weather-warning-system-for-keralas-western-ghats-region
**Confidence: High**

### Claim 13: "Punapuzha (Punnapuzha) bridge — sole access to Mundakkai — collapsed; Army built 190-ft Bailey bridge in ~31 hours"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED WITH A MINOR CORRECTION**

- The bridge that was destroyed crossed the **Punnapuzha river** (also called Punnappuzha; one of the tributaries of Chaliyar). Sources confirm the bridge collapse left "approximately 400 families stranded in Mundakkai and Attamala."
- **CORRECTION on name:** Sources describe it as the bridge over the Punnappuzha river at Chooralmala; it was a "100-ft long concrete bridge." The claim calls it the "Punapuzha bridge" — this name is not used in verified sources, which refer to it by river name or location. The bridge identity is correct; the name "Punapuzha bridge" may be non-standard.
- **Army Bailey bridge:** CONFIRMED — 190 feet long, 3m wide, built in 31 hours by 144 army personnel (Madras Engineer Group). Capacity: 24 tonnes. 19 steel panels. A companion footbridge (100 ft) was also built within 3 hours.

**Best source:** https://www.onmanorama.com/news/kerala/2024/08/01/wayanad-landslide-army-constructs-bailey-bridge-in-chooralmala.html
**Confidence: High**

### Claim 14: "BSNL restored 4G in Chooralmala/Mundakkai by afternoon of 31 July (~24–36h); Jio added a tower; Airtel free data"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED**

- **BSNL:** Restored 4G services in Chooralmala and Mundakkai **by the afternoon of July 31** — approximately 24–36 hours after the July 30 disaster. BSNL used diesel generators to keep towers operational during power outages.
- **Jio:** Increased network strength and set up an additional tower. CONFIRMED.
- **Airtel:** Provided 1GB free mobile data per day + unlimited calling + 100 SMS per day for three days to prepaid customers; extended bill payment dates for postpaid. CONFIRMED.
- Vodafone Idea (Vi) also provided 1GB free data per day for seven days.

**Best sources:** https://telecomtalk.info/bsnl-restores-4g-provides-free-calls-wayanad/979705/; https://www.deccanherald.com/india/kerala/telecom-operators-restore-augment-telecom-connectivity-in-landslide-hit-wayanad-3133367
**Confidence: High**

### Claim 15 (KEY): "The first 36 hours of Wayanad were defined by communication silence" — is it ONLY from Autoabode/MeshVani with no primary source?
**Verdict: CONFIRMED AS UNSUPPORTED — the phrase originates in Autoabode's marketing copy; no primary source found**

**Exact text from Autoabode's blog (autoabode.com/blog/lora-mesh-networking-disaster-relief-india):**

> "When the Wayanad landslides hit in July 2024, the first 36 hours of the rescue operation were defined less by manpower than by silence."

**No source is cited.** The Autoabode article provides zero reference, footnote, link, or attribution for this claim. It appears as an opening narrative hook for their product (MeshVani LoRa mesh communicator), not as a documented fact.

**What primary sources actually show:** BSNL restored 4G in Chooralmala/Mundakkai by **afternoon of July 31** — roughly 24–36 hours after the landslide. This means the Autoabode framing is at best an approximation of partial outage, and at worst contradicted by BSNL's documented restoration timeline. There was undoubtedly disruption, but characterising the full first 36 hours as "communication silence" is not supported by any government report, NDRF document, or credible news source.

**Assessment:** **Do not use this claim.** It is vendor marketing copy with no attribution, and it is potentially contradicted by BSNL's documented rapid restoration. The honest framing is: cellular infrastructure was disrupted in the immediate hours after the landslide; BSNL had 4G back in the affected areas within ~24–36 hours. The "36 hours of silence" is an unsupported narrative constructed by a company with a product to sell.

**Best source:** https://www.autoabode.com/blog/lora-mesh-networking-disaster-relief-india (the only source — and it cites nothing)
**Confidence: High** (high confidence that it is unsupported/vendor-sourced)

### Claim 16: "Victims were mainly tea/cardamom plantation workers, many migrant, with limited literacy; warnings didn't reach them"
**Verdict: CONFIRMED — with nuance on the "warnings didn't reach them" mechanism**

- **Plantation workers as primary victims:** CONFIRMED. Wikipedia, Down to Earth, and multiple sources state "most of the victims were tea and cardamom estate workers, asleep when the landslides struck." The villages of Mundakkai and Chooralmala are in plantation areas managed by companies like Harrisons Malayalam Limited.
- **Migrant workers:** CONFIRMED. "Inter-state migrant workers" are documented among victims; 41 plantation workers (20 dead, 21 missing) specifically noted in one report. A July 2026 Federal article confirms three killed in the 2026 follow-up Wayanad landslide were also identified as migrant workers.
- **Limited literacy / warning dissemination failure:** Partially confirmed. Academic literature (referenced in search results) notes "the population in many of these areas mainly consists of workers in tea and cardamom plantations, most of whom have limited educational backgrounds and lack disaster awareness." However, the *primary* warning failure was administrative (Hume Centre warning not acted on by the Collectorate, and the official warning at 10:35pm not mentioning evacuation) rather than solely a literacy/reach failure. The combination of both factors is accurate.

**Best sources:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Wayanad_landslides; https://pucl.org/manage-writings/wayanad-landslide-2024-a-human-rights-perspective-on-environmental-disaster-and-socioeconomic-fallout/
**Confidence: High**

## CRITICAL FLAGS FOR THE PITCH

**Claim #3 (satellite phones from Hong Kong):** Use with attribution — "India Today reported in June 2013 that..." Do not state as independently confirmed government fact. It is credible journalism but single-sourced.

**Claim #9 (NDMA "failed miserably"):** Do not use as a direct quote without finding the original India Today article text. Either drop the quote or attribute it generically to "critics and post-disaster analyses."

**Claim #15 (36 hours of communication silence):** DROP THIS CLAIM. It is unsupported vendor marketing from Autoabode/MeshVani. Using it exposes the pitch to easy debunking: BSNL's own press releases show 4G restored in ~24h. The honest, defensible framing is "immediate cellular disruption in the critical rescue window" — not 36 hours of silence.

**Claim #12 (Hume Centre warning):** Solid and multiply confirmed. The Collectorate's own RTI denial strengthens the narrative — the official position was that the warning *should not have been heeded* because it came from an unofficial channel. This is a stronger and more precise story than just "they didn't act on it."
