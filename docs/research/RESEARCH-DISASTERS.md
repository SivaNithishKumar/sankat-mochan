## 1. The one-paragraph thesis (what we claim, honestly)

Across India's major recent disasters, cellular networks **do** fail — but usually 4–24 hours in, from **power/diesel exhaustion**, not instant tower destruction. In that window, and in remote Himalayan/Western-Ghats terrain, coordination collapses: victims aren't located, effort is duplicated, and **migrant workers are deprioritized and linguistically excluded**. In Kerala 2018, ~300 volunteer ham-radio operators **improvised exactly what we propose to systematize** — off-grid relay + triage from one control room — and located 15,000+ people and enabled 1,800+ rescues. Sankat-Mochan systematizes that improvisation and adds an on-device multilingual AI triage layer for the migrant-language gap that repeatedly cost lives. **We are not claiming to replace the telecom network or fix governance — we close the information-coordination gap in the hours before professional restoration arrives.**

That sentence survives a brutal, India-knowledgeable judge. The overclaimed version ("the network dies instantly and we replace it") does not.

## 3. Event dossiers (India, cite-safe core facts)

### Kerala floods, Aug 2018 — the anchor case [CITE-SAFE]
- Worst in 100 years; 5.4M displaced; 1.24M in 3,274 relief camps; 483 dead.
- **All 14 districts lost connectivity for several days** — root cause diesel/battery exhaustion, not tower destruction. Chalakudy: all five operators (Jio, BSNL, Vodafone, Airtel, Reliance) failed at once, "couldn't send an SMS."
- Ham radio: 15,000+ located, 1,800+ rescued (see Beat 1).
- Fishermen rescued 65,000+ using 669 private boats, working through the night with headlamps.
- Migrant-worker exclusion (see Beat 2). Fake "dam collapsed" news spread in migrant WhatsApp groups → chaos.
- Sources: Wikipedia 2018 Kerala floods; TelecomLead https://www.telecomlead.com/telecom-services/kerala-floods-mobile-operators-fail-to-connect-during-disaster-85906 ; ARRL (above).

### Kedarnath / Uttarakhand, June 2013 — the command-chaos case [CITE-SAFE]
- 300,000 trapped; 580 dead, thousands missing; 110,000+ evacuated (Operation Surya Hope).
- Telecom at Kedarnath/Dharchula/Narayan Bagar **washed away**; BSNL later restored 272 towers + 44 exchanges.
- **Government imported 25 satellite phones from Hong Kong mid-operation** — vivid proof of preparedness failure. [CITE-SAFE — corroborated across telecomlead + Operation Surya Hope]
- Rudraprayag had **no district magistrate for 4 days**; Home Minister admitted "no coordination among agencies." Stranded people handed soldiers **physical lists of names/numbers** to pass to families — messaging when all networks were down.
- Sources: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Surya_Hope ; Down to Earth https://www.downtoearth.org.in/natural-disasters/heavens-rage-41497 ; UCL IRDR https://blogs.ucl.ac.uk/irdr/2022/04/01/the-kedarnath-tragedy-breakdown-or-breakthrough/

### Chennai floods, Dec 2015 — the last-mile demand case [CITE-SAFE]
- City cut off >1 week. Networks failed from tower damage + power loss + fuel shortage + BTS flooding + congestion.
- People wrote "please save me / send me a boat sir" — unable to call. Citizens built chennairains.org to crowdsource shelter/food.
- Ham radio (Indian Institute of Hams) activated a 7.070 MHz emergency net as first responders.
- Sources: ARRL https://www.arrl.org/news/radio-amateurs-respond-to-grim-flood-situation-in-southern-india ; Business Standard (telecom lessons).

### Wayanad landslides, 30 July 2024 — the warning + identification case [CITE-SAFE, with one caveat]
- 254 confirmed dead (some later counts higher), 118 missing; Mundakkai cut off; 93 relief camps.
- **A warning existed 16 hours early** (Hume Centre for Ecology) and was ignored because it "wasn't integrated with the official warning system." [CITE-SAFE]
- People survived by **self-evacuating on foot through forest in the dark**, guided by neighbours, past wild elephants — no signal, no alert. (Survivor accounts: Down to Earth / Onmanorama.)
- Victim ID crisis: bodies unidentifiable, 401 DNA samples cross-matched, graves numbered.
- **CAVEAT / DO NOT USE:** the "first 36 hours were communication silence" line is from a **LoRa-hardware vendor (Autoabode/MeshVani) with no primary source**, and contradicts the record — BSNL restored 4G in ~24h. The real chokepoint was **road/bridge access** (Punapuzha bridge collapsed), not the network. [VERIFY-FIRST → actually: DO NOT REPEAT the vendor claim.]
- Sources: Wikipedia 2024 Wayanad landslides; Deccan Herald telecom restoration https://www.deccanherald.com/india/kerala/telecom-operators-restore-augment-telecom-connectivity-in-landslide-hit-wayanad-3133367 ; Onmanorama lessons (above).

### Sikkim GLOF (South Lhonak), Oct 2023 — the no-early-warning case [CITE-SAFE]
- 55+ dead, 74+ missing; Teesta III dam destroyed; north Sikkim cut off (NH-10 collapsed).
- **No Early Warning System at the lake** despite GLOF concerns raised since 2005. Airtel's fibre suffered a major breakdown; repaired via survey drones.
- Root cause more governance (dam spillway capacity had been reduced 7,000→3,000 cumecs) than comms.
- Source: Mongabay https://india.mongabay.com/2023/10/no-early-warning-system-and-insufficient-dam-safety-turned-sikkim-flood-deadly/

### Himachal / Punjab monsoon, 2023 — the coordination-duplication case [CITE-SAFE]
- 428 dead; 5,748 landslides; 1,000+ roads blocked. Chamba/Kullu/Lahaul-Spiti telecom hit; DoT activated Intra-Circle Roaming.
- **Documented duplication of relief effort** from fragmented agency coordination; DDMAs "lacked current disaster response protocols, robust communication infrastructure, and trained personnel." Only **31 weather stations** operational in the whole state.
- Sources: DoT https://dgtelecom.gov.in/heavy-rains-and-landslides-disrupt-telecom-in-himachal-dot-steps-up-restoration-efforts/ ; Tribune/NDMA report https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/himachal/... (verify exact URL).

### Joshimath subsidence, Jan 2023 — the data-suppression case [CONTEXT ONLY, don't lean on it]
- Slow-onset; networks fine. ISRO subsidence report pulled from public site after NDMA barred publicizing. Illustrates "governance, not tech" — useful only as an honesty aside, not a value case.

## 4. The apex-authority backstop [CITE-SAFE — strong]

**NDMA itself built a satellite-based National Management Communication Network across 81 vulnerable
districts** (VSAT + ISAT satellite phones), explicitly for "failsafe communication" when terrestrial
networks fail. India's apex disaster authority officially concedes terrestrial comms can't be relied
on. Use this to pre-empt "does the network really fail?" skepticism.
Source: https://ndma.gov.in/Capacity_Building/Ops_Comm/IT_Comm_Project

## 5. The honest failure pattern (say this before a judge does)

Two mechanisms cause the preventable Indian deaths, and we should name both:
1. **A warning existed but wasn't delivered / acted on** (Wayanad 16h warning; Sikkim no-EWS; Kedarnath ignored IMD). — largely a **governance** failure.
2. **The network died in the 4–24h power-exhaustion window**, and coordination collapsed (Kerala, Chennai, Kedarnath). — the **comms/coordination** failure we address.

We address #2 and the *last-mile multilingual delivery* part of #1. We do **not** claim to fix a
district magistrate who doesn't show up for four days, a dam with a reduced spillway, or a bridge
that collapsed. Owning that boundary is what makes the rest credible.

## 6. Pre-loaded rebuttals (the brutal questions, answered India-only)

**Q: "Isn't the real problem governance, not technology? Your box doesn't make an official push the button."**
A: Correct, and we don't claim otherwise. Two India-specific answers: (1) our **sensor auto-fire needs no human authorization** — it raises the alarm even when officials don't, addressing the Wayanad "warning ignored" and Sikkim "no-EWS" pattern at the field layer; (2) the AI triage attacks the *documented* operational failures — Himachal 2023's duplicated effort and Kerala's migrant-language exclusion — which are execution gaps, not political ones.

**Q: "Networks don't actually die instantly in Indian disasters."**
A: Agreed — they degrade over 4–24 hours from diesel/battery exhaustion (Kerala, Chennai). That's exactly our window: rescue teams arrive in that window carrying our equipped nodes. NDMA built an 81-district satellite network for the same reason.

**Q: "Ham radio / fishermen already did this. Why an app?"**
A: They did — and it proves the demand. But it was improvised, unlanguaged, and unprioritized. We systematize the relay, add on-device multilingual voice triage (the migrant gap that got people killed), and auto-rank so a coordinator isn't reading 40 fragments by hand.

**Q: "Isn't this just a LoRa mesh — hasn't that been done?"**
A: The transport isn't the contribution and we won't pretend it is. The contribution is the on-device multilingual AI triage + the bidirectional dispatch loop + autonomous sensor corroboration, fused for the Indian migrant-worker reality. (Know the prior art — see RESEARCH-existing-solutions.md, "Stratos" — and claim the fusion, not the mesh.)
