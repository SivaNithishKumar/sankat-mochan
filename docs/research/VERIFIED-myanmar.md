## EARTHQUAKE BASICS

### Claim 1: 28 March 2025, magnitude 7.7–7.9, epicenter near Mandalay/Sagaing, ~500 km rupture; a 6.7 aftershock ~12 min later

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED**

- Date, time, and location confirmed: struck at 12:50:52 MMT, epicenter 14 km NNW of Sagaing city / 16 km west of Mandalay.
- Magnitude: Mw 7.7 is the peer-reviewed moment magnitude (GeoScienceWorld, USGS); some agencies reported Ms 7.9 / ML 8.2 — the 7.7–7.9 range is defensible.
- Rupture: ~500 km confirmed by satellite data (Wikipedia, ScienceDirect). Note one peer-reviewed paper (GeoScienceWorld) says ~480 km; "~500 km" is acceptable.
- Aftershock: Mww 6.7, occurred ~12 minutes after mainshock. CONFIRMED.
- **Confidence: HIGH**
- **Sensitivity: LOW**
- **Source:** Wikipedia 2025 Myanmar earthquake; GeoScienceWorld "The 28 March 2025 Mw 7.8 Myanmar Earthquake" (https://pubs.geoscienceworld.org/ssa/tsr/article/5/3/260/659444/)

### Claim 2: Struck ~12:50 PM local during Friday midday prayers

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED**

- Time confirmed at 12:50:52 MMT. Friday, 28 March 2025 was during Ramadan.
- Mosques had gathered worshippers for Friday Jumu'ah prayers (Al Jazeera, Tribune India). The timing during midday prayers is corroborated across multiple outlets.
- **Confidence: HIGH**
- **Sensitivity: LOW**
- **Source:** Al Jazeera "Hundreds of Muslims feared dead in Myanmar earthquake" (https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/3/29/myanmar-quake-muslims-feared-dead-destroyed-mosques)

### Claim 3: ~700 Muslim worshippers died in collapsing mosques; 600+ monks trapped (~80 died); 12-story condo collapse killed ~207; bank in Pyawbwe killed ~40

**VERDICT: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — contested figures for mosque deaths, rest confirmed**

**Muslim deaths (~700):** The Spring Revolution Myanmar Muslim Network estimated 700 Muslim worshippers killed across at least 60 damaged mosques. The All Myanmar Islamic Religious Organization estimated ~250 deaths at 50+ collapsed mosques; the junta put it at 500; DVB estimated potentially thousands. The figure of ~700 is sourced from a named advocacy org and is within the credible range — but CONTESTED between sources.
- **Sensitivity flag: MODERATE** — figures vary by source; use "up to 700" or "hundreds" to be defensible.

**600+ monks trapped, ~80 died:** CONFIRMED. Wikipedia (citing Myanmar Fire Services Dept): "Over 600 monks were trapped beneath the collapsed U Hla Thein Temple while taking examinations, 80 of whom died."

**12-story Sky Villa condo killed 207:** CONFIRMED. Wikipedia: "The 12-storey Sky Villa condominium building in Aungmyethazan Township progressively collapsed, killing 207 people." Note: the building is described as 12-storey, matching the claim.

**Kanbawza Bank (Pyawbwe) killed ~40:** CONFIRMED. Wikipedia: "A five-story Kanbawza Bank building collapsed there, killing all 40 occupants." Note: the bank building was five stories, not described as a separate "bank" floor count — the ~40 figure is confirmed.

- **Confidence (overall): HIGH for condo/monks/bank; MEDIUM for ~700 Muslim figure**
- **Sources:** Wikipedia; Al Jazeera (https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/4/1/myanmar-holds-minute-of-silence-as-death-toll-from-earthquake-tops-2700)

## CASUALTIES

### Claim 4: Deaths: Mizzima ~5,352 Myanmar (+103 Thailand, +1 Vietnam); junta ~3,770. Injured ~7,100–11,366. Missing ~538.

**VERDICT: MOSTLY CONFIRMED — junta figure needs correction**

- **Mizzima figure of ~5,352 Myanmar deaths:** CONFIRMED via search results citing Mizzima.
- **Thailand deaths (103):** CONFIRMED across multiple sources.
- **Vietnam death (1):** CONFIRMED.
- **Junta figure:** The claim says ~3,770. Search results show junta figures fluctuated: SAC spokesperson cited ~3,600 as of early April, with 5,017 injured and 160 missing. The ~3,770 figure may be a later update — PLAUSIBLE but the exact figure I found was ~3,600. Recommend verifying against a specific junta press release date.
- **Injured range 7,100–11,366:** CONFIRMED. Mizzima reported 7,108 injuries; DVB reported 11,366. The range is accurate.
- **Missing ~538:** CONFIRMED via Mizzima figure cited in search results.
- **Confidence: HIGH for Mizzima/Thailand/Vietnam; MEDIUM for exact junta figure**
- **Sensitivity: HIGH** — Independent vs junta figures are politically significant. Frame carefully: "junta-acknowledged figures were significantly lower."
- **Source:** Wikipedia; search results citing Mizzima, DVB, SAC spokesperson (Al Jazeera April 6: https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/4/6/rains-add-to-misery-in-quake-hit-myanmar-as-death-toll-rises-to-3471)

## COMMUNICATIONS & AID DELAY (LOAD-BEARING PITCH CLAIMS)

### Claim 6 (KEY): Sagaing region was already under a military-imposed internet blackout BEFORE the quake

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED**

- Multiple sources confirm pre-existing military communications blackouts in Sagaing, which was already a conflict zone between military and resistance forces.
- Search result (from a synthesis of the progressive voice / Rest of World sources): "In some of the worst-affected areas like Sagaing, Mandalay, Bago, and southern Shan State, phone and internet lines had already been severed even before the earthquake occurred. This was due to 'preexisting regime internet and mobile phone shutdowns for security reasons' implemented by Myanmar's military junta."
- Rest of World (April 3, 2025): "Myanmar's Sagaing region, the quake's epicenter and a battleground between the military and resistance forces, remains under a strict communications blockade."
- Myanmar Internet Project documented pre-existing shutdown orders covering 100+ townships (~1/3 of the country) before the quake struck.
- **Confidence: HIGH**
- **Sensitivity: MODERATE** — the cause is military policy (politically charged), but the FACT of pre-existing blackout is well-documented by multiple independent outlets. For the pitch, frame as "Sagaing was already in a communications dead zone" without dwelling on the political reason.
- **Primary sources:** Rest of World, April 3 2025 (https://restofworld.org/2025/myanmar-earthquake-internet-shutdown/); Progressive Voice/Myanmar Internet Project, March 29 2025

### Claim 7 (KEY): "Residents in blackout zones had to travel for several days to reach connectivity to call in their needs to aid groups"

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED — source identified, attribution is anonymous humanitarian worker**

- **Primary source:** Context/Thomson Reuters Foundation, April 9 2025, author Rebecca L Root (https://www.context.news/digital-rights/in-myanmar-internet-shutdowns-hinder-earthquake-aid-response)
- **Exact wording from Context/TRF:** "in areas with complete blackouts, residents have had to travel for several days to get connectivity to call in their needs to aid groups"
- Attribution: This is attributed to "a humanitarian worker" — it is NOT a named individual. The claim is credibly sourced from a working aid responder speaking to Context/TRF, but it cannot be traced to a named primary source.
- **Confidence: HIGH for the fact; MEDIUM for the exact attribution chain** (the humanitarian worker is anonymous)
- **Sensitivity: LOW-MODERATE** — factual, not politically disputed
- **Recommendation for pitch:** Use with attribution "according to a humanitarian worker quoted by Context/Thomson Reuters Foundation (April 9, 2025)." Do not present as a named primary source.

### Claim 8 (KEY): Aid took ~3 days to reach at least one town; most population uninformed of quake's extent for 24h+

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED for 3-day aid delay; PARTIALLY CONFIRMED for 24h+ uninformed**

- **3-day aid delay:** Context/TRF (April 9 2025, same humanitarian worker): "a lack of communications...meant three days passed before help reached his town." CONFIRMED from same source as Claim 7 — same anonymous humanitarian worker.
- **24h+ uninformed:** Context/TRF documents this as well; Myanmar Internet Project reported massive communication outages immediately post-quake across Yangon, Mandalay, Naypyidaw, Sagaing — making it effectively impossible for most people to know the full scale within 24 hours. The "24h+" claim is supported by the overall blackout picture but no single source states this exact figure with precision.
- **Confidence: HIGH for 3-day delay; MEDIUM for 24h+ figure (directionally correct but imprecise)**
- **Sensitivity: LOW-MODERATE**
- **Source:** Context/TRF April 9 2025 (https://www.context.news/digital-rights/in-myanmar-internet-shutdowns-hinder-earthquake-aid-response)

### Claim 9 (KEY): Starlink not licensed in Myanmar; soldiers CONFISCATED Starlink terminals in Sagaing area

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED — with important nuance on the confiscation claim**

- **Starlink not licensed:** CONFIRMED. Rest of World (April 3, 2025): "Starlink is officially not available in Myanmar." Context/TRF confirms it is not legally available.
- **Confiscation by soldiers:** CONFIRMED with nuance. The claim is sourced directly from Htaike Htaike Aung (Executive Director, Myanmar Internet Project) quoted in Rest of World (April 3, 2025):
  > "But we heard that when people are trying to use Starlink in this setting, the military or the soldiers come to confiscate the kits."
- **Critical nuance:** The wording is "we heard that" — this is a reported, second-hand account from an advocate, not a documented eyewitness event. It is credibly sourced (named expert, reputable publication) but is hearsay, not verified incident reporting.
- **Confidence: HIGH that Starlink is unlicensed/illegal; MEDIUM-HIGH that confiscation occurred (credibly sourced but explicitly reported as "we heard")**
- **Sensitivity: MODERATE** — The confiscation framing implicates the military directly. For the pitch, the stronger and safer claim is "Starlink is banned and anyone attempting to use it risks military seizure of the equipment" — which is fully supportable.
- **Source:** Rest of World April 3 2025 (https://restofworld.org/2025/myanmar-earthquake-internet-shutdown/)

### Claim 14: Junta continued airstrikes (72+) in weeks after quake; selective aid allocation to resistance-held areas

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED — with figures clarification**

- **Airstrikes:** Multiple authoritative sources confirm continued airstrikes after the earthquake. Wikipedia notes "From 28 March to 5 April, military airstrikes killed at least 68 civilians and members of rebel forces." A later Fortify Rights/Bellingcat figure indicates "since 13 April, at least 72 airstrikes." The "72+" figure thus refers to a later window (from April 13 onward), not the total since the earthquake.
  - If claiming "72+ airstrikes in the weeks after the quake" — this appears to be a combination of two different count windows. The pitch should be careful: "dozens of airstrikes documented in the weeks after the quake" is fully safe; "72+" requires specifying the counting window.
- **Selective aid allocation:** Amnesty International (April 2025), UN Special Rapporteur, and multiple outlets confirm military obstruction of aid to resistance-controlled areas.
- **Confidence: HIGH for continued airstrikes (well-documented by Amnesty, UN, Bellingcat, Fortify Rights); MEDIUM for the exact "72+" figure without specifying the time window**
- **Sensitivity: HIGH** — directly implicates junta. Factually well-documented but the pitch should handle carefully if the goal is to avoid making a political argument. Recommend framing as "independent monitoring documented dozens of airstrikes in quake-affected areas in the weeks following" with a cite to Amnesty/UN rather than leading with it.
- **Sources:** Amnesty International (https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2025/04/myanmar-inhumane-military-attacks-in-earthquake-areas-hindering-relief-efforts/); UN News (https://news.un.org/en/story/2025/04/1161866); Bellingcat (https://www.bellingcat.com/news/2025/04/29/open-sources-show-myanmar-junta-airstrike-damages-despite-post-earthquake-ceasefire/)

## KEY FINDINGS FOR PITCH USE

### SAFE TO USE AS-IS (high confidence, low sensitivity):
- Claims 1, 2, 5, 10, 12 — earthquake basics, population affected, internet cost figures, Htaike Htaike Aung quote
- Claims 6, 7, 8 — the load-bearing comms/aid delay claims are ALL confirmed; Claims 7 and 8 come from a single anonymous humanitarian worker quoted in Context/TRF — credible but cite the outlet directly rather than presenting as eyewitness primary data

### USE WITH CARE (contested figures or political sensitivity):
- Claim 3 (mosque deaths): use "hundreds" or "up to 700" — figures vary significantly by source
- Claim 4 (junta vs independent death toll): the disparity is real and documented; cite both with sources to show the gap, but note that independent figures can't be independently verified either
- Claim 9 (Starlink confiscation): the exact confiscation claim is "we heard that" per Aung — cite it accurately as reported/alleged, not as a documented incident. The stronger claim ("Starlink is banned and use risks seizure") is fully solid.
- Claim 14 (airstrikes): factually well-documented; politically sensitive. Use cautiously if pitch wants to avoid political framing.

### CORRECTED FIGURES:
- Junta death toll: use ~3,600 (not 3,770 — that exact figure wasn't confirmed; 3,471 was found in one April 6 Al Jazeera article)
- Airstrike "72+": this figure applies to post-April 13 count, not total since quake — use "dozens of documented airstrikes" or specify the window

### POLITICAL SENSITIVITY NOTE:
The entire communication failure story is inextricably linked to the military junta's deliberate shutdown policies. The pitch can truthfully say "communications infrastructure was unavailable" without leading with "because the military shut it down" — but any deep-background reader will draw that connection. Claims 6, 9, 11, 14 are the most politically loaded. For a product pitch, lean on the humanitarian/disaster-response framing and cite authoritative outlets (Context/TRF, Access Now, UN OCHA) rather than opposition media (Mizzima, DVB) for the key stats.
