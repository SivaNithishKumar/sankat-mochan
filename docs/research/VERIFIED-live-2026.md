### Claim 7 (KEY/LOAD-BEARING): Two-way satellite messengers (Garmin inReach, SPOT, Thuraya, etc.) ILLEGAL in India; travellers detained/arrested

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED — with precise legal basis and documented cases**

**Exact supportable facts:**
- **Legal basis:** Indian Wireless Telegraphy Act, 1933 (Section 6) and Indian Telegraph Act (Section 20). The 2023 Telecommunications Act is the most recent amendment. Any wireless apparatus capable of transmitting messages abroad requires prior government permission from the Department of Telecommunications (DoT). "Such permission is neither fast nor easy to obtain."
- **Devices affected:** Garmin inReach (all models including Mini), SPOT devices, Thuraya handsets, Iridium handsets, Inmarsat handsets, and any two-way satellite communicator. Receive-only GPS units and smartphones are permitted.
- **Airports:** Baggage scanners at Delhi, Mumbai, and all Indian airports specifically target these devices.
- **Documented cases (from ExplorersWeb, citing specific individuals):**
  1. Scottish trekker "Hiker_Heather" — detained at Delhi airport during domestic flight security screening after InReach placed in security tray; required a court appearance
  2. Canadian ultra-runner Tina Lewis — arrested at Goa airport with InReach Mini; released on bail with passport confiscated; incurred "thousands in legal costs"
  3. March 2025, Delhi IGI Airport — three American travelers and one Australian booked under Indian telecom laws for carrying Garmin GPS units and an Iridium satphone
- **Penalties:** Device confiscation + fine of ₹50,000–₹100,000 (payment required before leaving airport); prosecution can result in fines exceeding ₹100,000 and imprisonment

**Best sources:**
- ExplorersWeb (primary, detailed): https://explorersweb.com/do-not-bring-an-inreach-or-other-satellite-device-to-india/
- Air Traveler Club: https://www.airtraveler.club/intel/satellite-phones-illegal-india-declare-arrest/
- Himalaya Trekker: https://himalayatrekker.com/travel-tips/satellite-phones-garmin-inreach-spot-india-rules/

**Confidence: HIGH — legal basis and enforcement are well-documented. The March 2025 IGI Airport case is the most recent citable incident.**
