## SIKKIM GLOF (South Lhonak Lake), OCT 2023

---

### Claim 1 — Death toll (~55 dead / 74+ missing / ~92 later)

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED** (with chronological clarification)

**Exact supportable figures:**
- As of October 6, 2023: 55 dead, 75 missing
- By October 18, 2023: 92 confirmed dead (later count as bodies were retrieved)
- Among missing: 23 Indian Army personnel (7 subsequently found dead, 1 rescued alive)

**Best source:** ICIMOD press release + Science.org peer-reviewed study (2026)
- https://www.icimod.org/press-release/new-scientific-study-confirms-climate-change-played-key-role-in-deadly-2023-lake-outburst-in-sikkim/
- https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.ads2659

**Confidence:** HIGH. Multiple independent sources agree on these figures at each time point. "~92 later" is the final count, not the initial count.

### Claim 2 — Teesta III dam destroyed; north Sikkim cut off when NH-10 collapsed

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED**

**Exact supportable wording:** The 1,200 MW Teesta III hydropower dam at Chungthang was washed away on the night of October 3-4, 2023. Fifteen bridges were destroyed. The north of the state, including Gangtok, was cut off from the rest of India when parts of National Highway 10 collapsed.

**Best source:** SANDRP (South Asia Network on Dams, Rivers & People), October 4, 2023
- https://sandrp.in/2023/10/04/glacial-lake-flood-destroys-teesta-3-dam-in-sikkim-brings-wide-spread-destruction/

**Direct quote (Mongabay):** "The authorities were not immediately alerted that the lake had breached on October 4 because there was no Early Warning System (EWS) installed at the lake."

**Confidence:** HIGH.

### Claim 3 — KEY: "No Early Warning System at South Lhonak Lake" when it breached, despite GLOF concerns raised since ~2005

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED WITH NUANCE** (load-bearing claim — nuance matters)

**Exact supportable wording:** There was no functioning Early Warning System at South Lhonak Lake when it breached on October 4, 2023. A camera and weather instruments for a partial EWS had been installed in September 2023 — just weeks before the breach — but the full system was not yet operational. The NDMA acknowledged this gap by saying on October 4 it would "expedite" EWS deployment at at-risk glacial lakes.

**On the "since 2005" warning:** CONFIRMED. Scientists and citizens affected by Teesta river damming had warned about GLOF dangers since 2005. WAPCOS (a government engineering consultancy) acknowledged GLOF risks in a 2005 meeting but "chose not to assess the risk of GLOFs at all" in the Environmental Impact Assessment. The GLOF potential of South Lhonak Lake had been "long identified by researchers and notified to the government."

**Best source:** Mongabay India, October 2023
- https://india.mongabay.com/2023/10/no-early-warning-system-and-insufficient-dam-safety-turned-sikkim-flood-deadly/

**Direct quote (Mongabay):** "The authorities were not immediately alerted that the lake had breached on October 4 because there was no Early Warning System (EWS) installed at the lake."

**Important nuance for pitch:** One source (SANDRP) notes a camera was installed at the lake in September 2023, but the full EWS was not operational. The Mongabay/NDMA statement that there was "no EWS" is the authoritative characterisation. The system was "underway but incomplete" — effectively non-functional.

**Confidence:** HIGH for "no functioning EWS." HIGH for "warnings since 2005."

### Claim 4 — Teesta III spillway capacity had been reduced from 7,000 to 3,000 cumecs (governance failure)

**VERDICT: CORRECTED — REDUCTION WAS PROPOSED BUT BLOCKED, NOT IMPLEMENTED**

**What actually happened:** The dam developer (NHPC) *proposed* reducing spillway capacity from 7,000 to 3,000 cumecs after construction started. The Central Electricity Authority (CEA) and Central Water Commission (CWC) **blocked this reduction**. The final actual capacity remained at 7,000 cumecs.

**However**, 7,000 cumecs itself was inadequate — it was based on probable maximum flood from rainfall alone and did not account for a GLOF event. A CWC official is quoted: "7,000 cumecs is barely sufficient for rain-induced floods... A GLOF demands spillways of much higher capacity."

**The governance failure framing is still valid** but must be stated accurately: the failure was designing the spillway capacity at 7,000 cumecs without accounting for GLOF risk, not that the capacity was actually reduced to 3,000 cumecs.

**Best source:** Carbon Copy / SANDRP
- https://www.carboncopy.info/indias-new-hydel-push-cant-turn-blind-eye-to-teesta-iii-failures
- https://sandrp.in/2023/10/04/glacial-lake-flood-destroys-teesta-3-dam-in-sikkim-brings-wide-spread-destruction/

**Direct quote (SANDRP/Carbon Copy):** "Once construction started, it tried to unilaterally lower spillway capacity to 3,000 cumecs – a measure blocked by the CEA and the CWC."

**Confidence:** HIGH. The reduction-to-3,000-cumecs-was-implemented version is FALSE. Use the accurate version.

### Claim 5 — Airtel fibre suffered a major breakdown; repaired using survey drones (IG Drones)

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED**

**Exact supportable wording:** After the October 2023 Sikkim flood, Airtel's fibre optic network suffered a major breakdown. Airtel partnered with IG Drones, deploying drones to conduct aerial surveys to pinpoint the exact location of damage. IG Drones' specialised equipment then conducted the repairs, restoring the fibre optic network within an "exceptionally short timeframe."

**Best source:** Mobility India (citing Airtel directly)
- https://www.mobilityindia.com/how-airtel-is-leveraging-innovative-solutions-in-sikkim-to-restore-connectivity-affected-by-natural-calamities/

**Direct quote:** "The drones conducted aerial surveys to assess the extent of damage to the Airtel fibre optic line, providing crucial data for engineers to pinpoint the exact location of the damage... The specialised drone equipment, operated by IG Drones, accessed the damaged section of the fibre optic line with precision and speed, conducted necessary repairs."

**Note:** The article describes it as a flood (early October, "fuelled by relentless monsoon rains") without explicitly using the term "GLOF." Context makes clear this is the October 2023 event.

**Confidence:** HIGH.

## HIMACHAL PRADESH / PUNJAB MONSOON, 2023

---

### Claim 6 — "428 dead" in Himachal 2023 monsoon; ~5,748 landslides; 1,000+ roads blocked

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED WITH MINOR VARIATION ON DEATHS**

**Death toll:** The NDMA PDNA report cited ~500 lives claimed total; DD News reports 424 deaths (242 rain-related, 182 road accidents). The "428" figure is close to the 424 from DD News and within the range of official reporting; the NDMA PDNA round figure is ~500. The 428 figure is defensible; it may reflect a specific cutoff date.
- Source: https://ddnews.gov.in/en/himachal-monsoon-death-toll-rises-to-424-242-rain-related-182-in-road-accidents-sdma/

**Landslides:** 5,748 is CONFIRMED from the NDMA Post-Disaster Needs Assessment report, as cited by The Tribune India.
- Source: https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/himachal/himachal-pradesh-floods-climate-change-illegal-construction-greenery-loss-increasing-disaster-risk-in-himalayas-says-ndma-report-586698

**Roads blocked:** 1,000+ is CONFIRMED. Specifically, "more than 1,000 roads were blocked." On September 18, 604 roads including two national highways were still blocked.
- Source: 2023 North India floods Wikipedia / state government reports

**Confidence:** HIGH for landslides and roads. MEDIUM for 428 (range is 424-500 depending on source and cutoff).

### Claim 7 — DoT activated Intra-Circle Roaming (ICR) so any subscriber could use any network

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED**

**Exact supportable wording:** The Government of India's Department of Telecommunications directed telecom operators to activate Intra-Circle Roaming (ICR) in Himachal Pradesh during the 2023 floods. This allowed subscribers to latch onto any available network regardless of their primary operator, restoring connectivity across flood-affected regions.

**Best source:** Communications Today / DG Telecom
- https://www.communicationstoday.co.in/govt-issues-directive-to-start-intra-circle-roaming-in-himachal-pradesh/
- https://dgtelecom.gov.in/heavy-rains-and-landslides-disrupt-telecom-in-himachal-dot-steps-up-restoration-efforts/

**Confidence:** HIGH.

### Claim 8 — Documented "duplication of relief effort" from fragmented agency coordination; DDMAs lacked protocols/comms/trained personnel

**VERDICT: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED — DDMA failures confirmed; "duplication" not explicitly documented in available sources**

**What is confirmed:** Multiple sources confirm that DDMAs lacked current disaster response protocols, robust communication infrastructure, and trained personnel. Delays in inter-agency coordination and fragmented decision-making "severely hampered relief efforts." The scale of the disaster overwhelmed the administrative response.

**What is not confirmed:** The specific phrase "duplication of relief effort" does not appear in the sources reviewed. Fragmentation and coordination failure are documented; duplication as a distinct documented problem is not confirmed in available primary sources.

**Best source:** Tandfonline academic paper (Geospatial and statistical assessment) / NDMA PDNA report
- https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/27669645.2025.2543099
- https://recovery.preventionweb.net/publication/report-post-disaster-needs-assessment-himachal-pradesh-monsoon-2023-floods-cloudbursts

**Confidence for "DDMAs lacked protocols/comms/trained personnel":** HIGH.
**Confidence for "duplication of relief effort":** LOW — use "fragmented coordination" instead.

### Claim 9 — "Only 31 weather stations operational in all of Himachal Pradesh"

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED**

**Exact supportable wording:** The NDMA's post-disaster needs assessment report for the 2023 Himachal Pradesh monsoon stated that only 31 weather stations were operational in the state, describing the weather prediction and early warning capabilities as "limited." The NDMA recommended installing automatic weather stations in each gram panchayat and strengthening the early warning system.

**Best source:** The Tribune India, citing NDMA PDNA report
- https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/himachal/himachal-pradesh-floods-climate-change-illegal-construction-greenery-loss-increasing-disaster-risk-in-himalayas-says-ndma-report-586698

**Note:** Cross-reference: a state IPH department page (2018 data) shows 88 functional rain gauge stations of a different type (not AWS). The "31" figure from NDMA specifically refers to automatic weather stations, not all rain gauges — this is the meaningful metric for early warning.

**Confidence:** HIGH (from NDMA's own PDNA report).

## JOSHIMATH SUBSIDENCE, JAN 2023

---

### Claim 10 — ISRO/NRSC released a preliminary subsidence report (~5.4cm in 12 days) then REMOVED it after NDMA barred govt bodies from publicizing Joshimath data

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED — with precise chronology**

**Exact supportable wording:** The ISRO-NRSC released a report showing Joshimath had subsided 5.4 cm in 12 days (December 27, 2022 to January 8, 2023), with a "rapid subsidence event triggered on January 2." The report was taken down from the ISRO/NRSC website on January 13, 2023. On the same day (January 13), NDMA issued an Office Memorandum directing approximately a dozen technical/research bodies — including ISRO — to refrain from media interactions and from sharing data on social media about the Joshimath subsidence, without prior approval. The stated reason was that uncoordinated releases were "creating confusion."

**The suppression is confirmed but nuanced:** The Uttarakhand Cabinet Minister reportedly called the NRSC director asking if the report was their official position; NRSC responded it would be updated, and the report came down. NDMA's gag order formalised the media blackout that same day.

**Best sources:**
- Business Today: https://www.businesstoday.in/latest/in-focus/story/joshimath-crisis-isro-report-on-land-subsidence-missing-from-govt-website-360275-2023-01-14
- Outlook India: https://www.outlookindia.com/national/joshimath-crisis-government-experts-bodies-told-to-not-talk-to-press-congress-lashes-out-at-gag-order-news-253715
- India TV News: https://www.indiatvnews.com/news/india/joshimath-crisis-updates-ndma-directs-isro-government-agencies-to-refrain-media-interactions-on-without-pre-approval-congress-slams-order-2023-01-14-839304

**Direct quote (NDMA memo, paraphrased in multiple outlets):** "Various government institutions are releasing data related to the subject matter in social media platform, and also they are interacting with media with their own interpretation of the situation. It is creating confusion not only among affected residents but also among citizens of the country."

**Confidence:** HIGH. Both the removal of the ISRO report and the NDMA memo are confirmed by multiple independent outlets including The Wire, Business Today, Outlook, and India TV News.

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED (278 families); 930 people UNVERIFIED — actual figure is ~800-900**

## NDMA SATELLITE NETWORK

---

### Claim 12 — KEY: NDMA built a satellite-based "National Management Communication Network" (or similar) covering ~81 vulnerable districts with VSAT terminals + satellite phones, explicitly for failsafe comms when terrestrial networks fail

**VERDICT: CONFIRMED — with one name correction**

**Exact supportable wording:** The program is called **"National Disaster Management Services (NDMS)"** — not "National Management Communication Network." It covers **81 selected (vulnerable) districts** plus 36 state/UT headquarters and MHA/NDMA/NDRF HQ, totalling 120 locations. Each location was provided a fixed VSAT terminal (using BSNL's VSAT hub) and an ISAT (satellite) phone. The explicit rationale, in the official program description: *"The terrestrial communication network is prone to failure during disaster"* — the VSAT/satellite network provides failsafe communication for voice, data, video, and internet when terrestrial networks fail. The project was completed on March 31, 2019 and handed over to states/UTs. Implementing agency: BSNL. Cost: Rs. 19.54 crore.

**Official sources:**
- NDMA GoI: https://ndma.gov.in/Capacity_Building/Ops_Comm/IT_Comm_Project (returned 404 during verification — use Meghalaya SDMA page as mirror)
- Meghalaya SDMA (mirrors NDMA program description): https://msdma.gov.in/ndms.html

**Direct quote from program description:** "The aim of the project is to provide failsafe communication by facilitating voice/data communication amongst EOCs of vulnerable Districts, respective State HQs, MHA, NDMA, NDRF HQ and disaster sites through satellite in case of failure of terrestrial network due to any disaster."

**Confidence:** HIGH. The program, district count, VSAT terminals, satellite phones, and "terrestrial failure" rationale are all confirmed from official government sources.

**Name correction for pitch:** Use "National Disaster Management Services (NDMS)" not "National Management Communication Network."

## LOAD-BEARING CLAIM STATUS (as flagged)

- **Claim 3 (No EWS):** CONFIRMED. Safe to use with nuance: "no functioning EWS" is accurate.
- **Claim 10 (ISRO suppression):** CONFIRMED. Both the report removal and NDMA memo are confirmed by multiple independent news sources.

## ONE CRITICAL CORRECTION

**Claim 4 (Teesta III spillway):** The version "spillway capacity reduced from 7,000 to 3,000 cumecs" is FALSE. The reduction was proposed by the developer but **blocked** by CEA and CWC. The actual capacity remained 7,000 cumecs — which was itself inadequate because it didn't account for GLOF risk. Reframe as: "Despite regulators blocking a further reduction, even the 7,000 cumec design was insufficient — the spillway calculation excluded GLOF scenarios entirely."
